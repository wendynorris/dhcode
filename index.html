introduction

Welcome! Here's our roadmap for learning computational humanities

Hi. My name is Wendy Norris and I’m an Information Science PhD candidate at the University of Colorado Boulder.

With the help of the CU Boulder Center for Research Data and Digital Scholarship, I created this video series for friends, classmates, and fellow scholars who are interested in learning how to apply computational methods to their text-based research data.

These approaches are especially useful for very large corpora or messy datasets that need systematic cleaning before they can be analyzed with confidence.

In this video series there is no presumption of any prior experience. But maybe even more importantly, the focus is entirely on using computational methods for research--not making games or solving math problems.

In this case, we’ll be automating a typical qualitative text analysis approach by learning how to handle large amounts of data as well as how to conduct a basic sentiment analysis that will pass muster with Reviewer 2!
